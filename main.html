Maxima, Minima & Saddle Points

Interactive learning for multivariable calculus — visual and mathematical.

Maximum (Local/Global)

A point (a,b) is a local maximum if f(x,y) ≤ f(a,b) for points near it. Global maximum holds over entire domain.
✅ Condition: D > 0 and r = fxx < 0.

Minimum (Local/Global)

f(x,y) ≥ f(a,b) locally. Condition: D > 0 and r > 0.

Saddle Point

Critical point that is neither max nor min — surface curves up in one direction, down in another. Condition: D < 0.

Second Derivative Test

Let r = fxx, s = fxy, t = fyy, and D = rt − s².
➜ D>0 & r>0 → min   ➜ D>0 & r<0 → max
➜ D<0 → saddle   ➜ D=0 → inconclusive.
